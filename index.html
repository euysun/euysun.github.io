ABOUT

I am passionate about leveraging technology and policy to drive meaningful impact in national security and international relations. I’m currently a candidate for Stanford’s Master of International Policy program, specializing in Cyber Policy and Security. Most recently, I served as a Cybersecurity Analyst Intern at the Cyber Threat Alliance, where I supported international cybersecurity initiatives, member profiling, and policy engagement to enhance global data sharing standards. Before this, I spent over seven years as a diplomat with the South Korean Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA), where I led high-impact projects, including bilateral security cooperation with the U.S. and technology policy collaborations with scholars from Harvard and MIT. In these roles, I managed cross-functional teams, negotiated international agreements, and advanced public policy initiatives, focusing on aligning security strategy with technological advancements. At Stanford, I am taking courses to deepen my understanding of computer science, particularly in Artificial Intelligence, with a focus on exploring how AI can be applied to address real-world challenges.
